\title{Predicting Cross-Asset Stablecoin Contagion with Temporal Graph
Neural Networks}

\author{Anonymous}
\affiliation{\institution{Columbia University MAFN}\country{USA}}

\begin{abstract}
We study whether cross-asset stablecoin depeg contagion can be predicted from market
microstructure, using minute-level Binance and Coinbase data across seven real crisis
episodes (2022--2023). We frame contagion as per-node onset prediction on a temporal directed
graph of (asset, venue) nodes and evaluate a full model ladder---baselines, tabular
(XGBoost), sequential (GRU), and graph (GraphSAGE, GAT)---under a strictly leakage-safe
protocol in which same-window crises are held out together as clusters. Two findings emerge.
First, contagion \emph{onset} is unpredictable at horizons of four hours or less (every model
sits at the base rate) but becomes predictable at \textbf{24 hours}, where graph attention
(GAT) attains PR-AUC $0.447\pm0.016$ on the held-out SVB cluster, a margin of
$+0.175\pm0.016$ over XGBoost (5 seeds). Second, an edge ablation isolates \emph{where} the
signal lives: removing the directed lead-lag edges costs GAT $0.100$ PR-AUC but GraphSAGE only
$0.009$---the contagion signal is carried by the edges and exploited specifically by attention.
We close with an interpretable, exportable hub ranking that surfaces the venues central to each
crisis, intended as the actionable hand-off to downstream risk and policy analysis.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{stablecoins, financial contagion, temporal graph neural networks, graph attention,
systemic risk, leakage-safe evaluation}

\section{Introduction}
Stablecoins are systemically important and explicitly regulated (GENIUS Act, MiCA)
\cite{genius2025,mica2023}. When one stablecoin depegs, the question for a risk desk or a
regulator is which \emph{other} venues will follow, and how soon. Decentralized-exchange
contagion has been modeled with GNNs at the level of liquidity pools and bridge swaps
\cite{zhou2025bridge}; we ask the cross-asset, cross-venue analogue for stablecoins on the
deepest centralized venues, where the data is cleanest and the stakes (reserve runs) are
highest.

Our contributions are: (1) a \textbf{leakage-safe} temporal-GNN contagion predictor on real
multi-venue data; (2) a \textbf{lead-time} result---contagion is unpredictable at hours but
predictable at a day---reported with base-rate-free metrics; (3) an \textbf{ablation} that
isolates the marginal value of the graph structure (and shows it is attention-specific); and
(4) an interpretable, exportable \textbf{hub ranking} as the actionable output.

\section{Data and task}
We collect real 1-minute OHLCV from \textbf{Binance} (USDT-quoted: USDC, TUSD, USDP, FRAX,
BUSD, FDUSD, UST) and \textbf{Coinbase} (USD-quoted: DAI, USDT), peg-adjusting Binance prices by
the Coinbase USDT/USD mid so a USDT depeg is not masked by the quote currency. Seven episodes
span UST/Terra, FTX/DAI, the BUSD wind-down, USDC/SVB, FRAX/SVB, and the 2018/2022 USDT events
(Fig.~\ref{fig:depeg}).

A node is an (asset, venue) pair; a sample is one (hourly snapshot, active non-origin node).
The label is contagion \emph{onset}: node $j$ enters a new sustained stress period (asset-class
bps threshold, $\ge 10$ min) within horizon $\Delta$, having been calm at $t$, with the episode
origin excluded. We evaluate four horizons (30 min, 1 h, 4 h, 24 h).

\paragraph{Leakage control is load-bearing.} FRAX/SVB and USDC/SVB are the \emph{same}
March-2023 window; splitting them across train and test lets a model memorize the regime and
inflates XGBoost to PR-AUC $=1.0$. We therefore group same-window episodes into \emph{clusters}
and evaluate (a) a held-out SVB cluster and (b) leave-one-cluster-out (LOCO). This single design
choice is what separates an honest result from an artifact.

\section{Models}
A model ladder on identical samples: majority and persistence baselines; logistic regression
and XGBoost~\cite{chen2016xgboost} on per-node features; a GRU on per-node feature sequences;
and two GNNs on hourly directed snapshots---GraphSAGE~\cite{hamilton2017graphsage} and
GAT~\cite{velickovic2018gat}. Node features are IAQF-style microstructure signals (price ratio,
realized vol at 1\,h/24\,h, Amihud illiquidity, Kyle's $\lambda$, OU half-life, log volume) with
short lags. Edges are formed from a 6-hour rolling directed lead-lag graph; edge attributes carry
the signed lead-lag and correlation. GNNs predict per-node onset, masked to active non-origin
nodes, with class-weighted loss and early stopping.

\section{Results}
\paragraph{Lead-time.} Because PR-AUC rises mechanically with the base rate across horizons, we
report ROC-AUC (base-rate-free) and PR-AUC lift (Fig.~\ref{fig:leadtime}). At $\le 4$h no model
clears ROC-AUC $0.5$; at 24 h only the graph models do, GraphSAGE reaching ROC $0.65$. Contagion
onset is thus a \emph{day-scale} phenomenon in this data: the hour-scale negative result is real
and scopes the claim.

\paragraph{Headline (5 seeds).} On the held-out SVB cluster at 24 h, GAT attains PR-AUC
$0.447\pm0.016$, a margin of $\mathbf{+0.175\pm0.016}$ over XGBoost, which sits at the base rate
$0.29$. The margin is stable across seeds (a single unlucky seed gives $0.29$, so multi-seed
reporting matters). In LOCO the per-fold margins are smaller but positive
(Fig.~\ref{fig:loco}); dropping the degenerate single-positive USDT-2018 fold leaves GAT
$+0.083$ over XGBoost.

\paragraph{Ablation: where the signal lives.} Holding the architecture fixed and removing all
edges (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}) decomposes the gain over tabular into an \emph{architecture}
component (node-only XGBoost $0.271 \to$ GNN-no-edges $0.35$--$0.39$) and a \emph{graph}
component. The directed edges add $\mathbf{+0.100}$ PR-AUC for GAT but only $+0.009$ for
GraphSAGE: the cross-asset lead-lag structure carries genuine contagion signal, and \emph{graph
attention} is what exploits it. This directly answers the natural reviewer question---``is it the
graph or the features?''---and identifies attention over directed edges as the mechanism.

\section{Interpretation and the hub ranking}
The strongest microstructure precursors (XGBoost gain; Fig.~\ref{fig:features}) are the OU
half-life and 24-hour realized volatility---mean-reversion speed and recent turbulence, not the
price level. We combine GNN occlusion-influence (a perturbation-based, version-robust alternative
to GNNExplainer masks~\cite{ying2019gnnexplainer}), graph betweenness, and a \emph{non-circular}
propagator label computed from raw prices, into an exportable per-episode hub ranking
(Fig.~\ref{fig:hubmap}). For USDC/SVB the top hub is BUSD (highest betweenness and influence).
We deliberately stop at \emph{ranking}: whether a high-ranked hub is \emph{causally} responsible
for contagion---and the policy consequences of acting on a correlational ranking---is the subject
of a companion causal-validation study that consumes exactly this exported ranking.

\section{Limitations}
$n=7$ episodes on the cleanly fetchable cross-asset CEX universe; two are too thin to carry
signal. The lead-lag edges can over-credit thinly traded coins. Short-horizon contagion is
genuinely unpredictable here. The hub ranking is correlational by construction---its causal
status is out of scope here and addressed in companion work.

\section{Conclusion}
On real multi-venue data, day-scale stablecoin contagion is predictable and graph attention adds
real, edge-borne signal ($+0.10$ PR-AUC over the same architecture without edges), while
hour-scale onset is not. The method yields an interpretable, exportable hub ranking; how to act
on it safely requires asking whether its hubs are causal, which we take up next.
